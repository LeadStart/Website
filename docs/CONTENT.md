### Week-by-week Timeline
**Eyebrow:** Timeline
**H2:** What the first month looks like.
**Sub:** A predictable ramp — not a black box. Here's the week-by-week view of your first campaign.

- **Week 1 — Setup:** Domains purchased and configured (SPF, DKIM, DMARC). Inboxes spun up and enrolled in warming rotation. Intake call to finalize your service mix, target geography, and ideal customer profile.
- **Week 2 — Build:** Prospect list pulled fresh for your market, double-verified, and sliced into segments. Copy drafted in your voice, reviewed with you, revised, approved. Warming continues on all sending infrastructure.
- **Week 3 — First Sends:** Sequences begin at conservative volume. Early reply routing kicks in. First warm conversations start landing. We monitor deliverability daily and adjust in real time.
- **Week 4 — Ramp:** Send volume ramps to full cadence. Follow-up steps fire for non-responders. Nurture cadence activates for interested-but-not-ready leads. You've typically booked your first intro calls by now.
- **Month 2+ — Scale:** Campaigns hit full stride. We refresh prospect lists monthly so coverage stays current. Copy gets refined based on what replies are saying. Monthly check-in to review numbers and adjust strategy.

### Why this exists (3 paragraphs)
Commercial cleaning is an incredible business. Recurring revenue, sticky contracts, protectable margins. But the growth playbook most owners are handed — Google Ads, bid listings, and hoping referrals keep coming — quietly stops working somewhere around $1M–$5M in revenue.

That's the moment most cleaning companies start to plateau. Not because demand dried up, but because nobody ever taught them how to do outbound. And the "marketing agencies" who promise they can? They've never run a campaign in the cleaning vertical specifically. They'll sell you generic lead-gen and hand you a list that's been sold to your three biggest competitors.

LeadStart is what happens when you build an outbound agency that does only one thing: book qualified intro calls with property managers and facility directors, for commercial cleaning companies, with territory exclusivity and a guarantee that actually means something.
